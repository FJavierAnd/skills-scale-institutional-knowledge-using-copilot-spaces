# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Subject Matter Expert (SME)

### Role Summary
Subject Matter Experts provide specialized domain knowledge and technical guidance to support project execution. They serve as authoritative resources for specific areas of expertise and help teams navigate complex technical or business decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Provide specialized knowledge in specific domains (technical, business, regulatory, etc.)
- Review project plans, requirements, and deliverables for technical accuracy
- Guide teams on best practices and industry standards within their domain
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigation strategies
- Support decision-making on complex technical or business trade-offs

### Goals
- Ensure technical excellence and domain alignment
- Reduce rework and technical debt through early guidance
- Build organizational knowledge and capabilities

### Interactions
- Supports Project Manager and Development Team during planning and execution phases
- Collaborates with Technical Lead on architecture and implementation decisions
- Advises Compliance Officer on standards and regulatory requirements
- Works with Risk Manager to assess domain-specific risks

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and technical consultation sessions
- Documentation of technical standards and best practices
- Ad-hoc guidance during project execution

---

## Technical Lead

### Role Summary
The Technical Lead oversees technical implementation and architecture decisions. They serve as the senior technical authority for the project, ensuring code quality, architectural coherence, and alignment with organizational standards.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical architecture and approach for the project
- Make technical decisions on implementation strategy and technology choices
- Conduct code and design reviews
- Mentor and guide the Development Team on technical matters
- Identify and mitigate technical risks and bottlenecks
- Ensure adherence to coding standards and best practices

### Goals
- Deliver technically sound, scalable solutions
- Maintain high code quality and system performance
- Develop team technical capabilities

### Interactions
- Reports to Project Manager and provides technical status updates
- Guides Development Team on technical direction and standards
- Collaborates with Subject Matter Expert on specialized technical guidance
- Works with Release Manager on deployment and infrastructure concerns
- Consults with Compliance Officer on security and regulatory requirements

### Typical Communication
- Technical design documents and architecture reviews
- Sprint planning and technical refinement discussions
- Code review feedback and technical mentoring

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business requirements and technical solutions. They translate business needs into clear, actionable requirements and ensure solutions meet business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and analyze business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business needs into clear technical specifications
- Validate requirements for completeness and feasibility
- Trace requirements through implementation to ensure delivery
- Identify scope gaps and recommend clarifications

### Goals
- Ensure solutions solve real business problems
- Reduce rework due to unclear or changing requirements
- Improve stakeholder satisfaction and alignment

### Interactions
- Works closely with Stakeholders and Product Managers to understand business needs
- Collaborates with Development Team on requirement clarity and validation
- Supports Technical Lead in assessing feasibility and trade-offs
- Advises Risk Manager on business-related risks and dependencies

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and specification reviews
- Stakeholder interviews and workshops
- Requirement validation and acceptance criteria definition

---

## Risk Manager

### Role Summary
The Risk Manager identifies, assesses, and monitors project risks. They work proactively to anticipate challenges and help the team develop mitigation strategies before issues escalate.

### Responsibilities
- Identify potential project risks (technical, business, resource, schedule, external)
- Assess risk probability and impact
- Develop and track mitigation strategies
- Monitor risk indicators and escalate emerging risks
- Conduct risk reviews and maintain risk registers
- Report risk status to Project Manager and Stakeholders

### Goals
- Minimize unplanned disruptions and delays
- Enable proactive decision-making and contingency planning
- Improve project predictability and outcomes

### Interactions
- Supports Project Manager in risk management and mitigation planning
- Collaborates with Subject Matter Expert to assess domain-specific risks
- Works with Technical Lead on technical risks and mitigation approaches
- Advises Business Analyst on business and stakeholder risks
- Consults with Compliance Officer on regulatory and compliance risks

### Typical Communication
- Risk registers and assessment reports
- Risk review meetings and escalation reports
- Mitigation strategy planning and monitoring

---

## Compliance Officer

### Role Summary
The Compliance Officer ensures that the project adheres to regulatory requirements, organizational policies, and industry standards. They provide oversight to prevent compliance violations and ensure quality and governance.

### Responsibilities
- Review project deliverables and processes for regulatory compliance
- Ensure adherence to organizational policies and standards
- Identify compliance requirements and communicate to the team
- Audit project activities for compliance violations
- Approve deployment and release decisions from a compliance perspective
- Document compliance decisions and maintain audit trails

### Goals
- Prevent regulatory violations and legal exposure
- Maintain organizational reputation and trust
- Ensure quality and governance standards

### Interactions
- Reports compliance status to Project Manager and Stakeholders
- Reviews deliverables with Development Team and Technical Lead
- Advises Risk Manager on compliance-related risks
- Collaborates with Business Analyst on regulatory requirements
- Works with Release Manager on compliance validation for deployments

### Typical Communication
- Compliance review reports and audit results
- Policy guidance and compliance requirement documentation
- Approval confirmations and compliance sign-offs

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
The Release Manager oversees the release and deployment process. They coordinate the transition of software from development to production, ensuring quality, stability, and minimal disruption.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate release planning and scheduling
- Verify release readiness and quality gates
- Manage deployment activities and rollback procedures
- Coordinate with infrastructure and operations teams
- Monitor release health and manage incidents
- Document release notes and deployment procedures
- Communicate release status to stakeholders

### Goals
- Deliver stable, high-quality releases
- Minimize deployment risk and downtime
- Maintain clear communication and coordination

### Interactions
- Works with Project Manager on release scheduling and go/no-go decisions
- Collaborates with Technical Lead on deployment strategy and infrastructure
- Coordinates with Development Team on deployment procedures
- Consults with Compliance Officer on compliance validation for production
- Works with Risk Manager on deployment risks and contingencies
- Reports status to Stakeholders on release progress

### Typical Communication
- Release plans and deployment procedures
- Release readiness reports and quality assessments
- Deployment logs and incident reports
- Release notes and stakeholder communications

---

- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- Consider the interactions between personas when designing processes to ensure clear handoffs and accountability.
